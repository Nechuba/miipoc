Our Purpose

Our Mission

MIIPOC is a nonprofit that seeks to eliminate barriers faced by Black, Indigenous, and People of Colour in the field of planning.

We believe in creating a more inclusive and equitable planning profession through mentorship, community building, and professional development.

Our Programs

What We Do

6-Month Mentoring Program

Structured mentorship for BIPOC students and early-career professionals in the planning field.

Trust-Based Relationships

Mentorship Program

The program runs from January to June, providing mentorship for BIPOC students and aspiring planners in the GTA.

Become a Mentee

Mentees are early-career planners looking for guidance, community, and professional growth opportunities.

  • One-on-one guidance from experienced professionals
  • Access to exclusive workshops and networking events
  • Career development and job search support
  • Connection to a community of BIPOC planners

Become a Mentor

Mentors are experienced professionals committed to supporting BIPOC individuals transitioning into the planning field.

  • Make a meaningful impact in diversifying the planning profession
  • Develop leadership and coaching skills
  • Connect with other professionals committed to equity
  • Flexible time commitment (4-6 hours monthly)

Our Story

About MIIPOC

The Mentorship Initiative for Indigenous and Planners of Colour was established to address the under-representation of BIPOC in the planning field.

We believe mentorship is essential to building sustainable career paths and personal growth for BIPOC professionals. Our organization is dedicated to creating pathways that promote diversity, equity, and inclusion in urban planning.
